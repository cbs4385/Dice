# QUINTESSENCE
### A dice-placement game of elements and the cosmos

*Working rulebook — version 0.1 (playtest draft)*

**Players:** 2–4 · **Time:** ~30 minutes · **Ages:** 10+

---

## The idea

Each player is assembling a small corner of the cosmos on their own board, drafting elemental dice from a shared roll and slotting them into place. The five dice are the five Platonic solids, and each is tied to a classical element with its own value range — so a die is never just "high" or "low," it is a *reach* and a *risk*. The tetrahedron is cheap and dependable; the icosahedron is scarce and wild. Fill your board cleverly over six rounds and score the most points.

The single design idea underneath everything: **the five solids are not a power ladder, they are five risk profiles.** Small dice are reliable. Big dice are the only keys to certain cells, but they roll unpredictably. Learning to want the *right* die, not the biggest one, is the game.

---

## The five solids

| Element | Die | Solid | Face values | Bands it can reach | Role at the table |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Fire | d4 | Tetrahedron | 1–4 | Low | Plentiful, utterly reliable. Fills Low cells and Fire slots without risk. |
| Earth | d6 | Cube | 1–6 | Low, part of Mid | The workhorse. |
| Air | d8 | Octahedron | 1–8 | Low, Mid | Mid-range flex; the "average" die. |
| Aether | d12 | Dodecahedron | 1–12 | Low, Mid, High | Rare. One of only two keys to the High band. |
| Water | d20 | Icosahedron | 1–20 | Low, Mid, High, **Celestial** | Scarce and swingy. The **only** key to Celestial cells. |

**The value bands** every die is measured against:

| Band | Face values | Which solids can land in it |
|---|---|---|
| Low | 1–4 | any die |
| Mid | 5–8 | d6, d8, d12, d20 |
| High | 9–12 | d12, d20 |
| Celestial | 13–20 | **d20 only** |

## Components

- **60 elemental dice**, drawn from a single bag:
  - Fire (d4) ×16
  - Earth (d6) ×16
  - Air (d8) ×12
  - Aether (d12) ×8
  - Water (d20) ×8
- **1 draw bag**
- **4 player boards** (Ashfall, Tidewater, Zephyr, Bedrock)
- **6 Public Objective cards**
- **5 Private Element cards** (one per element)
- **The Firmament** — a small shared tray or printed area that holds leftover dice
- **Favor tokens** (12; each player starts with 3)

*(Counts are starting points — see Balancing levers at the end.)*

---

## Setup

1. Each player takes a **board** (deal randomly, or let players choose).
2. Each player takes **3 favor tokens**.
3. Shuffle the **Private Element cards** and deal one face-down to each player — this is your secret favored element.
4. Shuffle the **Public Objectives** and reveal **one**. It applies to everyone this game.
5. Put all dice in the **bag**. Set the **Firmament** in the middle, empty.
6. Give the start-player marker to whoever most recently looked at the stars (or pick randomly).

---

## The board

Every board is a **3 × 4 grid of 12 cells**. There are three kinds of cell:

- **Element cell** — only that element's die may be placed here (any face value).
- **Band cell** — any die may be placed here, but it only *scores* if its face falls in the band. An off-band die still legally sits there (and still counts for adjacency and objectives), it just earns no band points.
- **Wild cell (—)** — any die. Scores only through objectives and adjacency.

Every board carries exactly **one Low, one Mid, one High, and one Celestial** band cell, **four different element cells**, and **four wild cells** — same ingredients, different arrangement, so the boards are balanced but play differently.

## Playing a round

The game lasts **six rounds**. Each round has four steps.

**1. Roll.** The start player draws **(2 × players + 1)** dice from the bag and rolls them into a shared pool.
> 2 players → 5 dice · 3 players → 7 dice · 4 players → 9 dice

**2. Draft down.** Beginning with the start player and going clockwise, each player **drafts one die and immediately places it** on their board (see Placement).

**3. Draft back.** In reverse order (ending with the start player), each player drafts **a second die** and places it.

**4. To the Firmament.** Any dice left in the pool move to the **Firmament**, keeping their shown faces. Then pass the start-player marker one seat clockwise.

Over six rounds each player makes **12 placements** — exactly enough to fill a board.

### The Firmament

The Firmament is a growing reserve of past dice. On **either** of your two picks in a round, you may take a die from the Firmament **instead of** the fresh pool, using the face it already shows. This is your relief valve when the fresh roll is useless, or your way to grab a d20 that landed on a perfect 17 last round. Dice remaining in the Firmament at game end score nothing.

---

## Placement rules

When you draft a die you must immediately place it on an **empty cell** of your board, obeying all of these:

1. **Connection.** After your very first die, every die must be placed orthogonally *or* diagonally adjacent to at least one die already on your board. (Your first die of the game may go anywhere.)
2. **Element adjacency.** A die may **not** be orthogonally adjacent to another die of the **same element**. Diagonal same-element neighbors are fine.
3. **Cell lock.** Respect the cell: element cells take only their element; band cells take anything (scoring only in-band); wild cells take anything.

**If you cannot place any available die legally**, forfeit that pick — the placement is lost and a cell will stay empty (worth −2 at game end). Draft dice you can actually use, and lean on your favor tokens to avoid this.

---

## Favor tokens

You start with **3**. Spend one, at the moment you draft a die and before you place it, to do **one** of the following:

- **Adjust** — change the die's face up or down by **1 pip** (never below 1 or above the die's top face; no wrapping). This is how a d20 showing 12 becomes a 13 and unlocks a Celestial cell.
- **Reroll** — reroll the die you just drafted, then place it.
- **Defy** — place the die ignoring the **element-adjacency** rule this one time. (You must still obey Connection and Cell lock.)

**Unspent favor tokens are worth +1 point each** at game end, so hoarding is a real option.

## Objectives

### Public objective (one per game, shared)

1. **Firmament Rows** — 3 pts for each of your 3 rows with no repeated element.
2. **Deep Columns** — 3 pts for each of your 4 columns with no repeated element.
3. **Constellation** — 5 pts for each complete set of all five elements present anywhere on your board.
4. **Rising Tide** — 3 pts for each band cell you satisfy in the **Mid, High, or Celestial** band. *(Rewards risk.)*
5. **Elemental Bounty** — 2 pts for each element you hold **two or more** dice of.
6. **Scarcity's Reward** — 3 pts per **Water (d20)** and 2 pts per **Aether (d12)** die on your board.

*For a longer, chewier game, reveal two public objectives instead of one.*

### Private objective (secret, yours alone)

Your Private Element card names one element. Score **+2 points per die of that element** on your board at game end. (Note the tension: hoarding one element fights the element-adjacency rule and any "no repeats" public objective.)

---

## Game end & scoring

After the sixth round, boards are full. Total your points:

| Source | Points |
|---|---|
| Each **band cell** satisfied by an in-band die | **+4** |
| **Public objective** | as printed |
| **Private favored element** | +2 per matching die |
| **Unspent favor tokens** | +1 each |
| Each **empty cell** | **−2** |

**Highest total wins.** Tie-breakers, in order: (1) fewest empty cells, (2) most band cells satisfied, (3) shared victory.

### Worked example

Playing **Ashfall** (Board α) with the public objective **Deep Columns**:

- Band cells satisfied — you land in-band on Low, Mid, and Celestial (a d20 nudged to 15 with a favor token), but your High cell holds an off-band d8. → **3 × 4 = 12**.
- Deep Columns — columns 1, 3, and 4 have no repeated element; column 2 repeats Air. → **3 × 3 = 9**.
- Private element (Water) — you placed 3 Water dice. → **3 × 2 = 6**.
- Favor tokens — you spent 2, kept 1. → **+1**.
- Empty cells — none. → **0**.

**Total: 28 points.**

## Player counts & variants

- **2 players.** Works as written (5-die pool), but drafting is tight and the Firmament fills slowly. For more tension, add a "ghost" step: after the two draft passes, discard one random pool die to the bag before the rest go to the Firmament.
- **Solo.** Aim to beat a target score (start at 25). Roll 4 dice each round; keep 2, banish 1 to the Firmament, return 1 to the bag.
- **Draft the boards.** Instead of dealing boards, deal two to each player and let them choose one — adds a pre-game decision.
- **Edge start (Sagrada-style).** Require the first die to be placed on an outer cell. Adds an early constraint; makes the game slightly harder.

## Quick reference

**Round:** Roll (2×players + 1) → draft & place one (clockwise) → draft & place a second (reverse) → leftovers to Firmament → pass start marker.

**When you place, obey:** Connection · Element-adjacency · Cell lock.

**Bands:** Low 1–4 (any) · Mid 5–8 (d6+) · High 9–12 (d12, d20) · Celestial 13–20 (d20 only).

**Favor (×3):** Adjust ±1 · Reroll · Defy adjacency. Unspent = +1 each.

**Score:** satisfied band cell +4 · objective · private element +2 each · favor +1 each · empty −2.

## Design notes — the levers you can tune

This is a v0.1; here's where to turn the knobs when it doesn't feel right in playtesting.

- **Bag ratios** control scarcity and therefore how contested the big dice are. If Celestial cells feel impossible, add d20s; if big dice feel too easy, cut them. The current 16/16/12/8/8 makes the High/Celestial keys genuinely rare at four players.
- **Band cell value (currently a flat 4).** Flat keeps all four bands equally worth chasing, so no solid out-earns another — the d20's edge is *access*, not points. If you *want* big-dice boards to feel more rewarding, scale the value by band (Low 2 / Mid 3 / High 4 / Celestial 5) — but watch that it doesn't turn the game back into "biggest die wins."
- **Empty-cell penalty (−2).** This is the pressure that forces you to place awkward dice. Raise it to punish sloppy drafting, lower it to make the game gentler.
- **Favor tokens (3).** More tokens = more agency against bad luck and a lighter, friendlier game. Fewer = swingier and more tense.
- **Board difficulty.** Every board here has one of each band by design. For an easier family game, replace some High/Celestial cells with Mid/Low; for a brain-burner, add a second Celestial and let players fight harder over d20s.
- **Objective count.** One public objective keeps it light; two makes a richer ~45-minute game.

The core loop is deliberately small. Everything else — a zodiac subsystem on the Aether die, transmutation actions that upgrade a die up the solid hierarchy, seasonal scoring — is expansion territory once the base game feels good.
